How to read this
🟢 Nearby supported — bull spread tendency
MM actively adding longs. Since specs concentrate in nearby active contracts, their buying directly bids the front month relative to deferred. Flow dominated by MM = nearby outperforms = spread strengthens.
🟡 Neutral / watch
MM flow is not dominant in either direction. Producer hedging is present but not overpowering nearby. No clear spread bias from COT alone — use basis levels and seasonal carry structure as additional context.
🔴 Nearby under pressure — bear spread tendency
MM trimming or cutting longs. Speculative liquidation directly offers the nearby contract. Even if producers are still short, the MM unwind is the dominant force. Deferred months hold better during MM liquidation — spread weakens (N-Z goes more negative).
Key principle: Producer shorts alone do not determine spread direction. The critical variable is MM flow in the nearby — are specs adding or cutting? That is what moves N vs Z. The net flow differential (MM change vs producer change magnitude) shows which group is dominating price action in the front month.

How to read old crop vs new crop
Old crop — positions in contracts within the current marketing year (e.g. Jul-26 for corn). These reflect nearby storage, basis, and current demand dynamics.
New crop — positions in deferred contracts (e.g. Dec-26 and beyond). Heavy producer shorts here = forward selling of anticipated production = deferred offered.
MM spreading — calendar spread positions held by managed money. When this builds rapidly, specs are directly trading the old/new crop spread — a strong directional signal.
Spread implication — if producer shorts are more concentrated in new crop vs old crop, deferred is under more pressure. If MM is long old crop but short new crop (or vice versa), they are expressing a spread view directly.
